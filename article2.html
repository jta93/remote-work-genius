Top Noise-Canceling Headphones for Remote Work in 2025

Home Blog Digital Products About Contact

When your home office is noisy, a good pair of noise‑canceling headphones can make all the difference. We’ve summarized the top models of 2025 based on expert reviews and features.

Sony WH‑1000XM6 — Best Overall

The Sony WH‑1000XM6 remains a favourite for remote work thanks to its industry‑leading active noise cancellation, detailed audio and clear microphone performance. It supports pairing with multiple devices and boasts a long battery life, though it doesn’t offer a wired USB connection and comes at a premium price.

Pros

  • Excellent noise cancellation and sound quality
  • Comfortable fit for extended wear
  • Clear microphone performance for calls
  • Long battery life and multi‑device pairing

Cons

  • No USB wired listening option
  • Higher price than budget models

Learn more: Visit Sony’s product page

Dell Alienware Pro Wireless — Best with Boom Mic

Designed with gamers in mind, Dell’s Alienware Pro Wireless headset doubles as a solid option for remote work. It features a removable boom microphone, effective active noise cancellation and cushioned earcups for comfort during long calls. Battery life is respectable, but the earcups don’t fold flat and the headset isn’t compatible with Xbox consoles.

Pros

  • Removable boom microphone for clear voice capture
  • Good noise cancellation and battery life
  • Comfortable ear cushions

Cons

  • Earcups are not foldable
  • Not compatible with Xbox consoles

Learn more: See details on Dell’s website

Apple AirPods Max — Best Style

Apple’s AirPods Max combine a premium aluminium and mesh design with class‑leading active noise cancellation. They deliver rich, balanced sound and offer a spatial audio mode for immersive listening. The AirPods Max use Apple’s Lightning connector for charging and do not include a standard 3.5 mm jack, so they’re best suited for users fully invested in the Apple ecosystem.

Pros

  • High‑quality build and elegant design
  • Outstanding noise cancellation and transparency mode
  • Comfortable ear pads and headband

Cons

  • No standard 3.5 mm headphone jack
  • Uses Apple’s proprietary Lightning connector
  • Expensive compared to most competitors

Learn more: Discover the AirPods Max

Anker Soundcore Life Q30 — Best Budget

If you’re on a budget, the Anker Soundcore Life Q30 delivers surprisingly good active noise cancellation and battery life at a fraction of the cost of premium models. The headphones are lightweight and offer decent sound quality, but they lack touch controls and aren’t rated for water or dust resistance.

Pros

  • Very affordable price
  • Good noise cancellation and long battery life
  • Comfortable and lightweight

Cons

  • No touch controls
  • No dust or water resistance

Learn more: View the Soundcore Life Q30

Sennheiser Momentum 4 Wireless — Best Value

Sennheiser’s Momentum 4 Wireless offers a balanced combination of great sound quality, extremely long battery life and reliable Bluetooth connectivity. The active noise cancellation is competent, though not as advanced as some competitors, and the microphone’s noise rejection could be improved. Overall, it’s a strong value choice for remote workers seeking premium sound without the highest price tag.

Pros

  • Excellent battery life and sound quality
  • Stable connectivity with multiple devices
  • Competitive pricing for a premium brand

Cons

  • Noise cancellation isn’t as advanced as top competitors
  • Microphone noise rejection could be better

Learn more: Explore the Sennheiser Momentum 4

Conclusion

There’s a noise‑canceling headset for every need and budget in 2025. Whether you prioritise overall performance, a boom mic, premium design, affordability or battery life, one of the options above will help you stay focused in your remote office.
